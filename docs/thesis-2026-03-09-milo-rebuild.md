# Thesis: Milo Rebuild

**Date:** 2026-03-09

## Decision

Milo should stop presenting itself as a broad "AI systems for small business" firm on the core public site and instead lead with a sharper wedge:

**Agency owners and lean professional-service firms that already use AI casually, but still lose time and revenue to manual proposals, client reporting, follow-up, and research.**

This is the most credible bridge between Milo's actual offer, the market research, and the current asset base in the repo.

## Why This Wedge

### 1. It matches the strongest research signal

The research did not show an awareness problem. It showed an implementation gap. Agencies and professional-service firms are already experimenting with AI, but they still live in a messy middle ground:

- generic prompts
- inconsistent follow-up
- slow proposal turnaround
- client reporting that still depends on manual effort
- internal research that gets redone every week

These firms do not need to be told AI matters. They need help turning scattered AI usage into dependable owned systems.

### 2. It creates a sharper trust surface

"Small business AI systems" is too broad. It competes with software platforms, consultants, freelancers, and generic agencies all at once.

This wedge narrows the problem to a buyer who:

- feels operational drag every week
- already understands the value of AI in theory
- is close enough to the DIY ceiling to pay for implementation
- cares about ownership and transfer because the work is recurring and embedded in the business

### 3. It fits the current site better than a full category reset

The current site, content, and design system already support a serious, operational tone. Rebuilding around agencies and lean professional-service firms requires a message shift, not a design restart.

That keeps the rebuild fast and avoids artificial-looking design churn.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Stay broad around "AI systems for small business"
**Rejected.**

This keeps optionality, but it weakens trust and makes the offer feel abstract. The research consistently showed that broad positioning gets commoditized unless it is backed by very strong proof or distribution.

### Alternative 2: Lead with DIY automation rescue for all SMBs
**Rejected for homepage positioning.**

This is a valid acquisition angle, and it should show up in content and outreach. But as the primary site story it is still too horizontal and too close to generic automation shops.

### Alternative 3: Rebuild the whole site around executive AI enablement
**Rejected.**

Executive AI is real, but it is a different buying motion and a different trust model. It should stay as a secondary offer, not become the spine of the whole site.

## Core Strategic Claim

Milo should position itself as the team that turns scattered AI experiments inside agencies and professional-service firms into dependable owned systems for:

- proposals
- client reporting
- follow-up
- internal research

The copy should sound like an operator speaking to another operator, not an AI vendor speaking in abstractions.

## Rebuild Principles

1. **Keep the visual system.** No redesign. Preserve layout, hierarchy, motion, and the existing blueprint aesthetic.
2. **Narrow the copy.** Lead with buyer-specific language and workflow-specific pain.
3. **Avoid AI-generic phrasing.** No "transform your business" filler, no empty futurism, no soft startup language.
4. **Use customer-language pressure points.** Messy follow-up, slow proposals, manual reporting, scattered research, context switching, and the feeling of "we use AI, but not in a dependable way."
5. **Reframe pricing carefully.** `$399` should read as an entry point or narrowly scoped starting offer, not as a promise of unlimited custom build work.

## Pricing Thesis

The current low-end price can still work, but only if Milo stops implying that `$399` buys an open-ended custom system.

The offer should be interpreted as:

- a first workflow sprint
- a scoped implementation starting point
- a way to prove value on one recurring bottleneck before a larger build

The public site should make three things clear:

- the `$399` offer is narrow by design
- the real multi-workflow engagement starts at `$2,499`
- ownership, documentation, and handoff are part of the value, not vague add-ons

If Milo does not constrain the entry offer in the copy, the price creates margin risk and weakens perceived seriousness.

## Pages In Scope

- `index.html`
- `agents.html`
- `build.html`
- `demo.html`

## Specific Testable Outcome

Within **14 days** of launching the rebuilt wedge-specific positioning, Milo should achieve at least one of these outcomes:

1. **Qualified reply rate increases by 50%** versus the current broad positioning on comparable outbound outreach.
2. **At least 5 qualified intro calls** are booked from the first 100 high-fit agency / professional-service targets sent to the rebuilt site.

## What Counts as a Qualified Intro Call

A lead counts only if all three are true:

- The company fits the wedge: agency or lean professional-service firm
- They currently use AI or automation in some ad hoc way
- They describe at least one target workflow problem: proposals, client reporting, follow-up, or internal research

## What Would Falsify The Thesis

The thesis is weakened if:

- wedge-specific copy performs no better than the broad version
- the leads generated are curious but non-urgent
- most conversations still drift back to generic SMB automation instead of the chosen workflow cluster

If that happens, the next step is not to broaden again immediately. It is to test a second sharp wedge before reverting to generality.

## Immediate Strategy For The Rebuild

1. Re-anchor the homepage around the specific buyer and their workflow pain.
2. Turn the services page into a tighter "what we build for this kind of firm" argument.
3. Rework the build wizard so it recommends systems using buyer-relevant workflow language.
4. Update the demo page so the call feels like a wedge-specific working session, not a generic strategy inquiry.

## Launch Test Plan

The rebuild should not be treated as a brand exercise. It is a wedge test.

### Hypothesis

Sharper messaging for agencies and lean professional-service firms will convert better than the current broad SMB positioning because it matches a buyer with clearer urgency and better problem recognition.

### Traffic To Use

- outbound email to high-fit agencies and professional-service firms
- direct founder outreach
- any existing SEO or referral traffic that lands on the rebuilt pages

### Measurement Window

- 14 days from launch

### Success Threshold

At least one of the following:

- qualified reply rate improves by 50% on comparable outbound volume
- 5 qualified intro calls from the first 100 high-fit targets

### Failure Interpretation

If the wedge does not outperform, the likely lesson is not "the market is bad." The more probable explanations are:

- the wedge is still too broad
- the first workflow offer is not concrete enough
- outbound list quality is weak
- the trust story needs more proof than the site currently provides
