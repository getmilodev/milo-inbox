The Intake Problem Is Costing You Cases

Law firms have the highest cost-per-missed-call of any industry. A single unanswered call can represent tens of thousands in lost fees — and unlike a missed dental appointment, that client isn't calling back tomorrow.

80%
of legal prospects who reach voicemail never call back — they call your competitor

The numbers from legal marketing studies:

  • 80% of callers won't leave a voicemail for a law firm
  • 35-40% of prospective client calls come after business hours
  • 72% of potential clients will hire the first attorney who responds to their inquiry
  • Average time to respond to a new client inquiry at most firms: 24-48 hours. Average time a prospect waits before calling another firm: 5 minutes.

That last gap — 5 minutes vs. 24-48 hours — is where firms lose the most money. Speed of response isn't a nice-to-have. It's the single biggest factor in client acquisition.

Ethical Considerations: AI and Attorney-Client Privilege

Important: AI intake is not legal advice

An AI receptionist handles intake — gathering initial information, scheduling consultations, and screening for case type. It never provides legal advice, discusses case merits, or makes representations about outcomes. This is the same scope as a human receptionist or intake specialist.

Key ethical safeguards built into AI legal intake:

  • No legal advice: The AI is explicitly trained to never offer opinions on case viability, legal strategy, or likely outcomes
  • Confidentiality: Call data is stored on your infrastructure, not shared with third parties. You control the data.
  • Clear disclosure: The AI identifies itself as an automated assistant (configurable by jurisdiction)
  • Conflict checking: Can be configured to capture opposing party names for conflict screening before scheduling
  • Escalation protocols: Any call that touches legal substance gets flagged for attorney callback

The ethical bar for AI intake is actually lower than for human receptionists — because the AI follows its training precisely every single time. It won't accidentally give legal advice during a long conversation the way a paralegal or receptionist sometimes might.

How AI Legal Intake Actually Works

Scenario: Personal Injury, Tuesday 7pm

Caller: "I was in a car accident this afternoon. The other driver ran a red light. I'm at the ER now and someone told me I should call a lawyer."

AI: "I'm sorry to hear about your accident. I can help you schedule a free consultation with one of our attorneys. First, can I get your name?"

Caller: "Maria Rodriguez."

AI: "Thank you, Maria. A few quick questions so the attorney is prepared when they call you. Were there any other passengers in your vehicle? And do you have the other driver's insurance information?"

[AI gathers: date/time of accident, location, injuries, insurance info, opposing party name for conflict check, best callback time]

AI: "I have everything I need. One of our personal injury attorneys will call you by tomorrow morning at 9am. In the meantime, make sure to keep all medical records from tonight's ER visit. Is there anything else I can help with?"

Result: Attorney gets a structured intake summary: name, contact, accident details, injury type, insurance status, opposing party (for conflict check), urgency level. Response time: immediate vs. next-day voicemail check.

Scenario: Screening out non-viable cases

Caller: "I want to sue my neighbor for their dog barking."

AI: "I understand that's frustrating. Our firm focuses on personal injury and family law cases. For a noise complaint issue, you might want to contact your local city code enforcement or a general practice attorney. Would you like me to suggest some resources?"

Result: Non-viable call handled gracefully. No attorney time wasted. Caller gets helpful direction instead of voicemail.

What AI intake handles for law firms:

  • Case type screening: Identifies practice area match before scheduling
  • Intake questionnaire: Gathers key facts tailored to your practice areas
  • Consultation booking: Checks attorney calendars, books free/paid consults
  • Conflict capture: Records opposing party names for your conflict check system
  • Urgency scoring: Statute of limitations awareness, flagging time-sensitive matters
  • Bilingual support: English/Spanish intake (critical for immigration and PI firms)
  • Follow-up scheduling: Sends reminder texts before consultations to reduce no-shows
